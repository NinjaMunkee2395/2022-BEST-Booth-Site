This is our engineering design process.

This process allows us to formalize a method in order to have repeatable success.

Our first step is we define the problem. We do this by attending kick-off and reading the rules.

Next, we begin brainstorming and strategizing. We decide what we need for each task and brainstorm parts for each. Such as a traversal, arm, manipulator, and an arm for Squeaky, to name a few.

Once we have our potential pieces, we begin making CAD and prototypes for each. Just because something is prototyped or CADed does not mean we’ll use it. We do this to see what could work, and what won’t work.

Our fourth step is to choose our design. We do this by using our specialized design matrix. To set up our design matrix, we decide what qualities we want each component to have and how important these qualities are. We then rate each design on a scale of 1 to 10, in each of the categories, and multiply that number by a weight assigned to each quality based on importance.

We then begin construction. As shown here, our company takes precautions to ensure our employee’s safety. Brandon is wearing ear protection and safety glasses while monitoring the CNC. Ben is taking a safety test on the hand drill while Brandon is watching him.

Next, we test & modify.

As you can see, our process is in a circle. This is because we go through steps 1 through 6 multiple times throughout construction.

Finally, we have our final robot.
